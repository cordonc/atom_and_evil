Okay, so.

I pretty much lost my mind when I realized that I couldn't feel love or passion or anything really. I felt broken and frustrated, especially under the circumstances.

I've learned that brain chemically, I have had a major lack of dopamine being produced.

Dopamine basically lets you have emotions, or it causes emotional responses, that is. It also can highly affect your concentration levels, excitement, and just overall pleasure from everything.

So I felt mute because I wasn't feeling any good emotions, just all of the negative ones, such as sadness, anger, frustration, and anxiety.

I didn't know what was happening at the time and I was really quite ignorant towards mental illness and brain chemical stuff. I just thought I was broken.

I didn't know how to fix myself, I just couldn't do it no matter what I did, and eventually I became desperate and found myself addicted to anything that would stop all of the negative feelings for even just a moment. I became dependent, basically.

All of my life, really. I have had racing thoughts.

To give you an idea of what racing thoughts are like, it is like you are home alone and then you hear a really weird sound that is pretty loud and it sounds like it came from inside. What is going through your mind? "What was that? Is it a burglar? Or maybe a raccoon? Maybe something fell? Nah, but what if it is a burglar? What do I do? Think think think... Okay, I will go out of my room with my phone in my hand and I am going to go check it out, prepared to call 911 if there is anything unusual."

Now imagine thinking like that about everything, and I mean everything.

Constant.

Not very fun.

I like to explain the thought efficiency with like "10% of racing thoughts are actually useful, while 75% of normal, non racing thoughts are useful."

So it isn't like you are just thinking fast, there is a lot of clutter and ridiculousness, you need to go through 10 thoughts to get one useful one.

Anyways, I think I have explained that pretty well.

So, imagine having a crush on someone or just having someone in your life romantically. With normal thoughts you would think about them pretty often, right? But you can still go about your day normally without much disturbance.

But with racing thoughts, the often turns into a majority of the 90% of useless or unwanted thoughts going through your head.

Which basically means it is obsession. But you don't want to be thinking about them that much, and eventually you just don't want to think about them at all during your school day or whatever. It hinders most of your ability to focus and function normally because you get easily distracted and lose the 10% of useful thoughts.

The 10% of the thoughts are "they are wonderful" or something of that nature. The mushy thoughts that make you blush and feel butterflies. (You don't even get the butterflies without dopamine.) Then, on the other side, the 90% of the thoughts going through your head are straight up obsessive. You really don't want to be thinking about these things because it feels sick and crazy. "What are they doing? Who are they talking to? Are they thinking about me? I hope so. What did they have for lunch? Were they satisfied? I hope so. I need them. I hope they need me too."

Then you really start to lose any and all feelings for them as an actual independent person that you want to care about but cannot. Your thoughts manifest so quickly that eventually you start making up expectations and you fall in love with ideas, not the person. You care about your ideas and your expectations, while you believe you are caring about the person, but are really not at all.

You go out and do "romantic" things, going out of your way to do things to make them happy, or really, to make you happy.

Without the dopamine to let you do it naturally, the dependence and obsession becomes a straight up addiction.

Most of this is subconscious and well, at least for me, I didn't see anything wrong with it. I was so twisted, and legitimately sick in the head.

You get disappointed. You get sad. You just wanted to be happy, or not-sad.

You expect the other person to do the same things for you as well and you also begin to expect it.

You feel betrayed.
